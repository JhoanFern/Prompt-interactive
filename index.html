Context is Key

Set the scene. Provide background, define the audience, and clarify the situation.

Bad üëé

"Explain the HVAC system requirements."

AI Output: "HVAC systems require a certain airflow, measured in CFM. The system must also have appropriate ductwork, a thermostat, and a power source..."

Good üëç

"You are a senior mechanical engineer. Explain the HVAC requirements for our new 10,000 sqm fulfillment center to the finance team. Focus on the 'why' behind the specs (e.g., temperature control for groceries, energy efficiency) and avoid overly technical jargon."

AI Output: "For our new FC, the HVAC system is crucial for two main financial reasons. First, **product integrity**: we need different temperature zones, like a specific range for fresh produce and another for chilled goods. Failing to maintain these temperatures risks inventory loss, which is a direct hit to our bottom line. Second, **operational cost**: we are choosing energy-efficient units. While they might have a slightly higher initial cost, they will significantly lower our long-term electricity bills, leading to a faster return on investment."

List Instructions

Be specific and explicit. If a task has multiple steps, list them clearly.

Bad üëé

"What should I check for a new building?"

AI Output: "You should check the foundation, roof, walls, plumbing, and electrical systems. Also check for pests and the condition of the windows."

Good üëç

"Create a due diligence checklist for a potential new hub location. Break the list into three sections: 1) Structural Integrity, 2) Utility Connections (power, water, internet), and 3) Permitting & Zoning. Under each section, list at least 3 key items to verify."

AI Output:
**1. Structural Integrity:**
- Check for cracks in foundation...
**2. Utility Connections:**
- Verify 3-phase power capacity...
**3. Permitting & Zoning:**
- Confirm commercial zoning...

Examples (Show, Don't Tell)

Provide examples of your desired output style, tone, or format. This is called "few-shot prompting".

Bad üëé

"Write an email to the contractor about the delay."

AI Output: "Subject: Delay. Hi, The project is delayed. Please advise. Thanks."

Good üëç

"Draft a formal email to our primary electrical contractor about a 2-week delay in the delivery of switchgear. Maintain a professional and collaborative tone. For example: 'We're writing to provide an update...' not 'You need to be aware...'"

AI Output: "Subject: Update on Switchgear Delivery for Project FC12. Hi [Contractor Name], We're writing to provide an update on the switchgear delivery for the FC12 project. We've been notified of a 2-week delay... Let's connect to discuss any impact..."

Ask the AI to "Think"

For complex tasks, instruct the AI to reason step-by-step before giving the final answer. This improves accuracy.

Bad üëé

AI Output: "Yes, installing solar panels is a good idea for the environment and can save money."

Good üëç

Think step-by-step: analyze initial CAPEX, available government subsidies, average annual sunlight, projected energy cost savings, and the estimated payback period. Then, conclude with a final recommendation."

Refine & Iterate

Treat prompting as a conversation. Your first prompt is rarely your last. Refine based on the output.

You: "Create a project plan for a new hub fit-out."

→ AI gives a generic plan with phases like 'Planning', 'Execution', 'Closing'.

You (Refined): "That's a good start. Now, break down the 'Execution' phase into more detailed sub-tasks specific to our fit-outs, like 'Flooring', 'Shelving Installation', and 'Electrical'. Add a column for estimated duration in days for a 2,000 sqm hub."

Establish Constraints

Guide the AI by setting boundaries for format, length, style, and what to include or exclude.

Role (Define the Persona)

Assigning a role helps the AI adopt the appropriate tone, style, and knowledge base.

Bad üëé

"Critique this project plan."

AI Output: "The project plan seems okay. The timeline is a bit short. The budget could be higher. The tasks are listed."

Good üëç

"Act as a devil's advocate. I am providing a project plan for the new FC10 fit-out. Your task is to critique it by identifying three potential risks related to supply chain or contractor availability that I may have overlooked. Be critical but constructive."

AI Output: "Constructive critique of the FC10 plan: **1. Supply Chain Risk:** The plan relies on a single supplier for shelving; a delay there could halt the entire project. **2. Contractor Risk:** Scheduling electrical work before HVAC is complete could lead to rework... **3. ...**"

Your Prompting Playbook

Precision Unlocks Potential. Think surgeon, not sledgehammer. The more thoughtfully you craft your prompt, the deeper you tap into the AI's capabilities.

Context is Your Compass. Without it, the AI is navigating blind. Provide the 'who, what, why' to guide it to your desired destination.

Structure Shapes Success. Defining the format and constraints ensures you get what you need, in the way you need it.

Iterate, Don't Abdicate. Prompting is a skill. If the first result isn't perfect, refine your instructions. Don't just give up.

"A vague prompt is like an IKEA manual with no pictures. You'll get a result, but it won't be the bookshelf you wanted, and you'll have screws left over."
